How it works

Two traditions, thousands of years of healing

Acupuncture

Acupuncture is powerful medicine that helps strengthen the immune system, prevent disease, control pain, and improve quality of life. It is a well-developed system of whole health care based on natural energetic laws. Dating back over 3,000 years as a primary health care system in China, acupuncture is now widespread in Asia, Europe, and the U.S.

The practice uses fine needles and pressure on specific “points” along pathways, or meridians, that cross the body. As long as energy flows freely through these pathways, health is maintained. When that flow is deficient, interrupted, excessive, or blocked, the result is illness or pain. Stimulating the appropriate points releases and regulates that energy so health can be restored.

Studies indicate acupuncture influences the central and peripheral nervous system, and evidence shows it releases endorphins from the brain — making it particularly effective for pain control. It also affects blood sugar, cholesterol and triglyceride levels, the gastrointestinal system, and the endocrine system.

Chinese Herbal Medicine

Alongside acupuncture, Dr. Hu practices herbal medicine — the main treatment method within Traditional Chinese Medicine. TCM is the world’s oldest continually practiced professional medicine, with a written history stretching back over 2,500 years. It uses naturally available herbs from our environment, rather than synthesized chemicals, keeping side effects to a bare minimum.

Although acupuncture was the first Chinese treatment to gain wide acceptance in the west, Chinese herbal medicine is quickly establishing itself as one of the most popular and effective alternative therapies here.

Diagnosis is made from the subtle signs and symptoms your body reveals. Dr. Hu feels the subtle variations in the pulse at your wrist to understand how each organ is functioning. The sound of your voice; the appearance of your tongue, face, and skin; and your emotions are all important indicators. Herbs are then formulated specifically for your diagnosis.
